01. The Gap

We trust science to drive human progress, but the current system is broken. Billions of dollars in funding are wasted trying to build on results that were never solid to begin with.

  • The "Replication Crisis" Over 70% of published experiments cannot be independently reproduced by other scientists.
  • The PDF Problem In 2026, we still share complex, data-driven discoveries as flat, static documents. It’s like reviewing software by looking at screenshots.

02. Executable Research

Think of it as GitHub meets Academic Publishing.

The Modern Research Publication & Simulation System (MRPS) turns static papers into executable software. Instead of reading a claim, a reviewer clicks "Run." The system spins up a simulation, feeds in the data, and proves the math works.

The Verification Breakthrough

The biggest barrier to open science is privacy (e.g., patient records or IP).

Our system acts as a neutral third party. We verify the process, not just the raw data. It runs code inside a secure "black box," confirms the results match the paper, and issues a "Verified" stamp—without ever exposing sensitive data.

03. The Knowledge Map

From isolated papers to an interconnected global brain.

Science today is fragmented. A biologist in Tokyo might not know that a chemist in Berlin has solved a key piece of their puzzle. MRPS changes this by building a semantic map in the background.

Cross-Domain Interlinking

As research is uploaded, the system automatically identifies connections across disparate fields (e.g., Physics linking to Biology). It creates a living graph of knowledge, revealing hidden dependencies that human researchers might miss.

Intelligent Partnership

The system proactively suggests collaborations. If you upload a model that aligns with another ongoing study, MRPS alerts both parties: "Potential Synergy Detected." This turns competition into collaboration.

The Path to Autonomous Science

By structuring data into a universal map, we are laying the foundation for AI agents that can traverse this graph, hypothesize new connections, and eventually conduct autonomous "in-silico" experiments.

04. Autonomy

The infrastructure for autonomous science.

The future of discovery isn't human-only. MRPS is the foundational protocol for the next decade of automated research.

By moving away from static, human-readable PDFs to a dynamic, machine-readable context layer, we provide the native environment where autonomous AI agents can seamlessly query, execute, verify, and publish structured scientific knowledge.

05. Sector Impact

Pharma & Biotech

Risk Reduction. Before licensing a discovery, companies can use our secure sandboxes to validate the computational model works as claimed, without requiring the university to hand over full IP/datasets immediately.

AI & Machine Learning

Reproducible Benchmarks. MRPS treats research as containerized software. This ensures that an AI model claiming 95% accuracy can be run in an identical environment, eliminating "it works on my machine" issues.

Academic Institutions

Reputation & Compliance. The system automates compliance with strict open-science mandates (FAIR principles) and acts as a "pre-flight check" to prevent high-profile retractions.

Public Policy

Auditable Evidence. Policymakers receive interactive simulations, not static PDFs. They can stress-test assumptions (e.g., "What if inflation rises by 2%?") directly within the verifiable research object.

07. Evidence

The MRPS architecture is not theoretical. It is built upon verified research into the crises of reproducibility and the future of autonomous discovery.

Validating the "Reproducibility Crisis"
The Nature Survey (2016) ↗

Confirmed that 70% of researchers have failed to reproduce another scientist's experiments. Identified "unavailable code/data" as a primary cause.

Cancer Biology Reproducibility (2021) ↗

Only 46% of high-impact cancer studies could be successfully replicated, validating the urgent need for our pre-clinical verification sandboxes.

Psychology Replication Project (2015) ↗

Found that only 36% of psychology studies could be replicated, galvanizing the movement for Open Science mandates.

Executable Research & FAIR Principles
The Case for Executable Papers (Science, 2011) ↗

Established that "the code and data are the actual scholarship," and the PDF is merely the "advertisement." This is the core philosophy of our Interface Layer.

The FAIR Guiding Principles (2016) ↗

The global standard (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) that mandates data must be machine-actionable. MRPS automates this compliance.

Jupyter Notebooks as Publications (2016) ↗

Validated that interactive, code-embedded documents significantly improve peer review and understanding compared to static text.

Autonomous Discovery & AI
The Robot Scientist "Eve" (2015) ↗

Proved that AI systems can autonomously hypothesize and execute experiments if data is structured correctly in a Knowledge Graph (our Phase 3 goal).

Knowledge Graphs for Drug Repurposing (2020) ↗

Validates that linking disparate data types (drugs, diseases, targets) in a network graph allows AI to predict new uses for old drugs—exactly the "Cross-Domain Innovation" use case MRPS supports.

The Semantic Web (2001) ↗

The foundational vision of making data "machine-readable." MRPS implements this by mapping simulation parameters to ontologies.

Technical & Privacy Validation
BioContainers (2017) ↗

Shows that using Docker containers for scientific tools (like the COPASI engines used in MRPS) solves dependency hell and ensures long-term reproducibility.

Synthetic Data in Clinical Trials (2021) ↗

Demonstrates that synthetic data (statistically identical to real data but containing no real patient info) can be used to validate models without privacy leaks.

W3C PROV-O Standard ↗

The international standard for interchange of provenance information. MRPS uses this for "Cryptographic Provenance" to ensure audit trails.
